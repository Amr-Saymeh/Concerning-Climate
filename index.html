Climate Change is Real

The evidence is clear and undeniable. Here are some key facts about our changing climate:

Rising Temperatures

The planet's average surface temperature has risen about 2.12 degrees Fahrenheit since the late 19th century, with most of the warming occurring in the past 40 years.

i

Reducing your carbon footprint by just 20% could help slow global warming. Try using public transportation or carpooling!

Melting Ice Sheets

Greenland and Antarctic ice sheets have decreased in mass. Data from NASA's Gravity Recovery and Climate Experiment show Greenland lost 279 billion tons of ice per year between 1993 and 2019.

i

Using energy-efficient appliances can reduce the energy demand that contributes to global warming and ice sheet melting.

Rising Sea Levels

Global sea level rose about 8 inches in the last century. The rate in the last two decades is nearly double that of the last century and accelerating yearly.

i

Conserving water reduces energy used for pumping and treatment. Take shorter showers and fix leaky faucets!
