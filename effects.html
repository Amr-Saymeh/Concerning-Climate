The Effects of Climate Change

Climate change impacts every corner of our planet, from melting ice caps to extreme weather events, threatening ecosystems and human communities alike.

Global Impacts

The effects of climate change are already visible worldwide and will continue to intensify if action is not taken to reduce greenhouse gas emissions.

Current and Projected Effects

Climate change is affecting our planet in numerous ways:

Rising Temperatures

Global temperatures have increased by about 1.1¬∞C since pre-industrial times. This warming is causing heat waves to be more frequent and intense, affecting human health, agriculture, and infrastructure.

i

Urban areas experience higher temperatures than surrounding rural areas due to the heat island effect, making cities particularly vulnerable to heat-related health issues.

Sea Level Rise

As ice sheets and glaciers melt and ocean water expands due to warming, sea levels are rising. This threatens coastal communities, infrastructure, and ecosystems with flooding and erosion.

i

By 2100, global sea level is projected to rise by 0.3 to 1.2 meters, putting over 800 million people at risk of coastal flooding.

Extreme Weather

Climate change is increasing the frequency and intensity of extreme weather events like hurricanes, floods, droughts, and wildfires, causing loss of life, displacement, and economic damage.

i

The economic cost of extreme weather events has increased five-fold over the past 50 years, with damages exceeding $100 billion annually in recent years.

Ocean Acidification

As oceans absorb CO2, they become more acidic, threatening marine life, especially shell-forming organisms like corals and mollusks. This impacts entire marine ecosystems and the people who depend on them.

i

Ocean acidity has increased by about 30% since the industrial revolution and could increase by 150% by 2100 if emissions continue at current rates.

Biodiversity Loss

Climate change is altering habitats and seasonal patterns, forcing species to migrate or adapt. Many cannot keep pace with these changes, leading to population declines and extinctions.

i

Up to one million plant and animal species are at risk of extinction due to human activities, including climate change, according to the UN.

Food Security

Changing precipitation patterns, rising temperatures, and extreme weather events threaten agricultural productivity in many regions, potentially leading to food shortages and price increases.

i

For every 1¬∞C rise in global temperature, grain yields decline by about 5%, threatening food security for millions of people.

Human Impact

Climate change affects human communities in profound ways, often disproportionately impacting vulnerable populations:

Human Health

Climate change threatens human health through heat-related illnesses, expanded ranges for disease vectors like mosquitoes, worsened air quality, and mental health impacts from extreme events and displacement.

i

The World Health Organization estimates that climate change will cause approximately 250,000 additional deaths per year between 2030 and 2050.

Migration & Displacement

Rising seas, extreme weather, and resource scarcity force people to leave their homes. By 2050, up to 143 million people could become climate migrants, creating humanitarian challenges and potential conflicts.

i

Currently, more people are displaced by climate-related disasters than by conflict, with an average of 21.5 million people displaced annually.

Economic Impacts

Climate change damages infrastructure, reduces worker productivity, disrupts supply chains, and increases costs for healthcare and disaster recovery. These impacts could reduce global GDP by up to 18% by 2050.

i

The cost of inaction on climate change is estimated to be 3-10 times greater than the cost of reducing carbon emissions.

Regional Effects

Climate change impacts vary by region, with some areas experiencing more severe effects than others:

Arctic

The Arctic is warming at twice the global average rate, causing rapid ice melt, thawing permafrost, and threatening indigenous communities and wildlife. This melting also contributes to global sea level rise.

Small Island Nations

Rising seas and intensifying storms threaten the very existence of low-lying island nations, which face existential threats from inundation, saltwater intrusion into freshwater sources, and coastal erosion.

Urban Areas

Cities face threats from heat island effects, flooding, and infrastructure damage. By 2050, over 570 coastal cities will face sea level rise by at least 0.5 meters, putting over 800 million people at risk.

There's Still Time to Act

While the effects of climate change are serious and far-reaching, there is still time to mitigate the worst impacts through collective action and policy changes.
